# Purpose

This document defines the Knowledge Graph Model for the Gildmark portfolio.

The Knowledge Graph represents the portfolio as an interconnected body of governed knowledge rather than a collection of independent documents.

Its purpose is to make architectural concepts, engineering practices, implementation artifacts, evidence, and capability claims explicitly connected, navigable, and traceable.

The Knowledge Graph is the structural representation of the portfolio's knowledge architecture.

---

# Architectural Philosophy

Knowledge is not hierarchical alone.

It is relational.

Although documents are organized into a governed hierarchy, architectural understanding emerges from the relationships between concepts, decisions, implementations, evidence, and professional capabilities.

The Knowledge Graph captures those relationships explicitly.

---

# Definition

The Knowledge Graph is the authoritative model describing the relationships among all governed portfolio artifacts.

Each artifact becomes a node within the graph.

Relationships between artifacts become governed edges.

Together they represent the architectural structure of the portfolio independently of its physical organization.

---

# Objectives

The Knowledge Graph exists to:

* preserve conceptual relationships
* support traceability
* improve navigation
* expose architectural dependencies
* connect evidence to capability claims
* reveal architectural lineage
* enable multiple portfolio views

The graph is therefore a representation of knowledge rather than documentation.

---

# Knowledge Nodes

Every governed artifact is represented as a knowledge node.

Examples include:

* concepts
* principles
* architectural decisions
* architectural patterns
* architecture documents
* engineering documents
* realization documents
* implementation artifacts
* evidence artifacts
* capability claims

Nodes represent authoritative knowledge.

---

# Knowledge Relationships

Knowledge nodes participate in explicit relationships.

Representative relationship types include:

* derives from
* governs
* realizes
* supports
* depends upon
* references
* demonstrates
* traces to
* supersedes

Relationships communicate architectural meaning rather than document organization.

---

# Graph Characteristics

The Knowledge Graph exhibits several defining characteristics.

## Governed

Relationships are intentional.

Every relationship has architectural meaning.

---

## Directed

Knowledge flows through explicit dependency.

Authority moves from governing concepts toward realization and evidence.

---

## Traceable

Every significant capability claim can be traced to supporting evidence through the graph.

---

## Extensible

New artifacts become additional nodes.

Existing architectural relationships remain stable.

---

## Technology Independent

The Knowledge Graph is a conceptual model.

It may be realized through documents, relational structures, graph databases, semantic technologies, or future representations.

The architectural model remains unchanged.

# Navigation

The Knowledge Graph supports multiple navigation paths.

Readers may navigate by:

* architectural concept
* governing principle
* architectural decision
* knowledge domain
* implementation artifact
* capability claim
* representative evidence

Every navigation path ultimately resolves to the same governed knowledge base.

---

# Architectural Benefits

Representing the portfolio as a Knowledge Graph provides several advantages.

These include:

* explicit conceptual relationships
* complete architectural traceability
* simplified portfolio navigation
* reduced duplication of knowledge
* multiple presentation views
* durable architectural lineage
* extensibility as the portfolio evolves

The graph transforms documentation into a coherent knowledge system.

---

# Scope

This document defines the conceptual model of the Knowledge Graph.

It does not define:

* graph schema
* node taxonomy
* relationship taxonomy
* storage technology
* visualization techniques

These concerns are addressed in subsequent Knowledge Graph documents.

---

# Relationship to the Portfolio

The Governance documents define how portfolio artifacts are created and maintained.

The Evidence domain establishes how architectural claims are substantiated.

This document connects those domains by defining the portfolio as a governed network of related knowledge rather than a collection of independent documents.

Subsequent Knowledge Graph documents define the ontology, schema, traceability, dependency model, and capability mappings that complete the portfolio's knowledge architecture.
