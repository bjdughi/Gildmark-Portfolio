# Purpose

This document identifies the representative artifacts selected to demonstrate the principal architectural, engineering, and leadership capabilities embodied within the Gildmark portfolio.

The objective is not to enumerate every project artifact.

Instead, the document highlights a curated set of evidence that collectively illustrates the architectural approach, engineering methodology, and professional capabilities developed throughout the project.

---

# Selection Philosophy

The portfolio contains substantially more material than most reviewers will have time to evaluate.

Representative artifacts provide an intentionally curated path through the strongest evidence.

Artifacts are selected because they demonstrate architectural judgment, engineering discipline, or technical communication more effectively than alternative examples.

Selection emphasizes quality of evidence rather than quantity of work.

---

# Selection Criteria

Representative artifacts should satisfy one or more of the following criteria:

* establish a foundational architectural concept
* demonstrate architectural judgment
* capture a significant design decision
* illustrate recurring architectural patterns
* exemplify engineering governance
* realize architectural concepts through implementation
* communicate complex ideas with clarity

Artifacts selected for this document are expected to remain stable reference points as the portfolio evolves.

# Using This Document

Readers seeking a rapid evaluation of the portfolio may begin with the artifacts identified in this document.

Each representative artifact serves as an entry point into a broader body of supporting evidence.

Cross-references within each artifact provide direct paths to related concepts, decisions, engineering practices, and implementation examples.

---

# Evolution

Representative artifacts may change as the portfolio matures.

Selection should remain intentional.

An artifact should be replaced only when another provides stronger evidence of the same capability or demonstrates a new capability more effectively.

Historical artifacts remain valuable as evidence of architectural evolution even when they are no longer designated as representative.
