# Purpose

This document defines the organization of evidence within the Gildmark portfolio.

The Evidence Catalog provides a structured index of representative artifacts supporting the architectural, engineering, and professional capability claims presented throughout the portfolio.

Rather than listing every artifact produced during the project, the catalog identifies the evidence most relevant to evaluating architectural judgment and engineering maturity.

---

# Evidence Philosophy

Evidence should be discoverable.

Readers should not be required to infer architectural capability by browsing source code or unrelated documentation.

Instead, evidence is organized around the questions a reviewer is most likely to ask.

The catalog therefore groups artifacts according to the capability they demonstrate rather than the repository in which they reside.

---

# Evidence Organization

Evidence is organized into five categories.

```text
Architectural Evidence
        │
Engineering Evidence
        │
Implementation Evidence
        │
Validation Evidence
        │
Evolution Evidence
```

Each category contains representative artifacts demonstrating a distinct aspect of the project.

---

# Architectural Evidence

Demonstrates architectural thinking and design.

Representative artifacts include:

* Architectural Principles
* Architectural Decision Records
* Lifecycle Governance Platform
* Operational Truth
* Operational Truth Engine
* State-Driven Operations
* System Architecture
* Architectural Patterns

These artifacts demonstrate conceptual architecture and architectural judgment.

---

# Engineering Evidence

Demonstrates engineering methodology and governance.

Representative artifacts include:

* Engineering Methodology
* Engineering Governance
* Contract-First Engineering
* Engineering Validation
* Architectural Evolution
* Engineering Handoff
* High-Level Build Plan
* Technical Debt Register

These artifacts demonstrate disciplined engineering practice.

---

# Implementation Evidence

Demonstrates realization of architectural concepts.

Representative artifacts include:

* service contracts
* representative service implementations
* lifecycle models
* state machine implementations
* API contracts
* endpoint inventories

Implementation evidence illustrates how architectural decisions are expressed in software.

---

# Validation Evidence

Demonstrates confidence in architectural and engineering correctness.

Representative artifacts include:

* validation methodology
* representative test scenarios
* seeded business workflows
* lifecycle verification
* consistency reviews

Validation evidence demonstrates disciplined verification rather than exhaustive test coverage.

---

# Evolution Evidence

Demonstrates architectural learning and refinement.

Representative artifacts include:

* Architectural Decision Register
* superseded designs
* document refactoring
* governance improvements
* architectural revisions
* design rationale

These artifacts demonstrate the progressive maturation of the architecture.

---

---

# Evidence Selection

The portfolio intentionally emphasizes representative evidence.

Selection criteria include:

* architectural significance
* clarity of design intent
* demonstration of engineering judgment
* traceability to architectural principles
* applicability across multiple domains

The objective is to demonstrate quality of reasoning rather than quantity of implementation.

---

# Traceability

Every evidence artifact should trace back to the architectural model established within the portfolio.

Readers should be able to navigate:

```text
Capability Claim
        │
        ▼
Evidence Artifact
        │
        ▼
Architectural Decision
        │
        ▼
Architectural Principle
        │
        ▼
Architectural Concept
```

This traceability reinforces the portfolio's evidence-based structure.

Different audiences may begin with different evidence.

| Audience             | Recommended Starting Evidence                                              |
| -------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| CTO                  | Portfolio Thesis, System Architecture, Architectural Decisions             |
| Principal Engineer   | State-Driven Operations, Service Architecture, Engineering Methodology     |
| Enterprise Architect | Lifecycle Governance Platform, Operational Truth, Architectural Patterns   |

The catalog supports multiple entry points while preserving a coherent architectural narrative.

---

# Scope

This document defines how evidence is organized and presented.

It does not replace individual evidence artifacts or summarize their contents.

Each artifact remains the authoritative source for its own architectural or engineering topic.

---

# Relationship to the Portfolio

The Evidence Model establishes the principles governing evidence throughout the portfolio.

This catalog organizes that evidence into a navigable structure aligned with the portfolio's capability claims.

Subsequent documents present the individual evidence artifacts and their relationship to the architectural concepts, engineering methodology, and implementation decisions they substantiate.
